S&P 500 IPO Performance

The first 30 days after a company goes public are usually a period of high volatility as the market tries to figure out what the stock is actually worth. While the initial "IPO pop" on day one gets the most attention, that single day doesn't always show the whole picture of how a company eventually becomes an S&P 500 leader.

This project breaks down that transition through four specific views: The Distribution shows the overall market behavior and how common underpricing actually is across all IPOs. The Box Plot identifies sector-level trends to see which industries are the most unpredictable and which are more stable. The Scatter plot tests the correlation between that first-day return and where the stock ends up after a month. Finally, the Cumulative chart tracks the actual paths of individual companies to show exactly how specific S&P 500 firms performed during their first few weeks of trading.

Select a view from the navigation menu to start exploring the data.
